BattleBot

A group of friends and I are designing a battle bot to fight in a competition against other bots in the hopes we win the tournament. The division we are aiming for is the beetle weight divison, or about 3 pounds. After a lot of research we have decided to go with a four wheel design for better movement and stability, and a standard beater bar for attacking while staying within our weight limit. Above is a photo of the final product chassis that will be made of metal and plastic to meet weight requirements without sacrficing protection for internals.

Seen above is the figure for our wheel controls and the general layout for the wiring of our bot. We plan on using tank controls for steering since it's cheaper, weighs less, and is safer for protecting our wheels. We also plan on driving wheels on each side with one gear box in order to reduce weight. As for the internal electronics they're fairly simple. A receiver will receive transmissions from a controller and send them through ESC's to the gearboxes, all powered by a LIPO battery. The ESC's are necessary for controlling the speed and steering of the bot.

Currently we have a prototype built with 3d Printed wheels attached to the gearbox, reciever, and battery on a small chassis made of scrap metal and cardboard. It can drive but doesn't steer and isn't ready for final product. We will likely have to make another prototype with all four wheels and ESC's to make sure our design works, then put it in the chassis we designed as our final product.
